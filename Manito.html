Manito Municipality

Manito is a coastal municipality in the province of Albay.The municipality has a land area of 107.40 square kilometers or 41.47 square miles which constitutes 4.17% of Albay's total area. Its population as determined by the 2020 Census was 26,162. This represented 1.90% of the total population of Albay province, or 0.43% of the overall population of the Bicol Region. Based on these figures, the population density is computed at 244 inhabitants per square kilometer or 631 inhabitants per square mile. Manito has a total of 15 barangays. The household population of Manito in the 2015 Census was 24,707 broken down into 5,101 households or an average of 4.84 members per household. The population of Manito grew from 3,798 in 1903 to 26,162 in 2020, an increase of 22,364 people over the course of 117 years. The latest census figures in 2020 denote a positive growth rate of 1.21%, or an increase of 1,455 people, from the previous population of 24,707 in 2015.The municipal center of Manito is situated at approximately 13° 7' North, 123° 52' East, in the island of Luzon. Elevation at these coordinates is estimated at 24.6 meters or 80.8 feet above mean sea level.
